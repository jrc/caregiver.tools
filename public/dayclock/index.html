Day Clock

Day Clock is a free web app that shows the day, time, and date in a way that’s easy to understand for people with cognitive disabilities. It’s also designed to work on older phones and tablets, giving new life to unused devices. Caregivers can remotely set a custom message to be displayed on the clock.

Hardware Requirements

You need an unused phone or tablet to serve as the dedicated Day Clock.

Day Clock has been tested on iOS 9, and all iOS devices introduced since 2011 can be upgraded to iOS 9 or later. To check the version of iOS on a device, go to the Settings app > General > About and look for Version.

Day Clock may also work on Android and even older Apple iOS devices, but it has not been tested. Let me know if you have any success.

Installation

Perform the steps below on the device that will serve as the Day Clock.

Erase the Device (optional)

It is recommended, but optional, that you completely erase the device, so that no other apps interfere with the operation of the Day Clock.

Your device will be erased. Make sure you have a copy of any data that you want to keep.
  1. Go to the Settings app > General > Reset. Tap “Erase All Content and Settings”, then “Erase” to confirm.
  2. Set up the device as a new iPhone/iPad, with no passcode, Apple ID, or Siri:
    • Choose your language, country, and Wi-Fi network.
    • Tap “Enable Location Services” (optional).
    • Tap “Passcode Options”, then “Don’t Add Passcode”, then “Continue”.
    • Tap “Set up as New iPhone/iPad”.
    • Tap “Don’t have an Apple ID or forgot it?”, then “Set Up Later in Settings”, then “Don’t Use”.
    • Tap “Agree” to the Terms and Conditions.
    • Tap “Turn On Siri Later”.
    • Tap “Send to Apple” (optional).
    • Tap “Share with App Developers” (optional).
    • Finally, tap “Get Started”.

Prepare the Device

  1. Go to the Settings app > General > Accessibility > Guided Access (it’s near the bottom), and set Guided Access to On.
  2. Go to the Settings app > General > Auto-Lock, and choose Never.

  3. Press the Home button, then go to the Day Clock app.
  4. Triple-click the Home button, then tap Start to enter Guided Access (single app mode).

Put the Day Clock into Place

  1. Position your Day Clock as desired for the person who will use it. As the device can be confused for a normal phone or tablet, you may wish to use tape or other means to fasten the device in place.
  2. Make sure the device is plugged in and charging.
